Diabetes - a common and prevantable serious disease
Did you know that 1 in every 20 canadians has or will have diabetes?
What is diabetes?
Diabetes Type 1
  • Between 5%-10% of people with diabetes have this form
  • Usually diagnosed in childhood
  • The causes are unknown
  • The body doesn't produce insulin
  • Patients need insulin daily for the rest of their lives
Diabetes Type 2
  • Approximatelly 90% of all cases of diabetes
  • Usually occurs in adulthood
  • Body doesn't produce enough insulin and/or the cells don't react to it
  • It can be controlled by a health diet and an active life style
  • It is a progressive disease, gradually getting worse
Pre-diabetes
  • Approximatelly 80 million of people are diagnosed with pre-diagnosed each year worldwide
  • Long-term damage to body, including heart and circulatory system
  • Starts with unhealthy eating habits and inadequate exercise

How to prevent Diabetes
Diabetes Prevention I
  • Type 2 Diabetes is highly preventable
  • Maintain a healthy body weight
  • Exercise regularly and keep active
  • Researchers estimated that there were 176 cases of type 2 diabetes per 100,000 individuals per year for every 2 hours of television watching per day in the U.S.
Diabetes Prevention II

  • Avoiding processed meat, carbohydrates, sugary foods and drinks and limiting red meat
  • Mono and polyunsaturated fat
